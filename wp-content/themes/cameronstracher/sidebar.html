Cameron Stracher grew up on Long Island, went to college at Amherst, law school at Harvard, and got his MFA from the Iowa Writers Workshop. His first novel, The Laws of Return, was published by William Morrow in 1996 while he was working as litigation counsel at CBS. His non-fiction account of his life as a law firm associate, Double Billing: A Young Lawyer's Tale of Greed, Sex, Lies, and a Swivel Chair, was also published by Morrow in 1998. He left CBS in 1999 and joined the media law firm Levine Sullivan Koch & Schulz. In 2001, he began teaching at New York Law School, and & Schulz. In 2001, he began teaching at New York Law School, and eventually became the Publisher of the Law Review and the Co-Director of the school's new Program in Law & Journalism. His second book of non-fiction, Dinner with Dad, was published by Random House in 2007 and optioned for television by 3Arts Entertainment. In 2011, Sourcebooks published his first YA novel, the dystopian thriller The Water Wars. In 2013, his non-fiction book about running, Kings of the Road, was published by Houghton Mifflin.

Cameron Stracher is the author of five books, including Double Billing. A graduate of Harvard Law School and the Iowa Writer’s Workshop, he has taught and practices media law, and written for The New York Times and The Wall Street Journal, among many other publications. He lives with his wife and two children in Westport, CT.
